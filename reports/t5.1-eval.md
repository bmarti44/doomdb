# T5.1 portal and sector timeline evaluator candidate

Status: **APPROVED under standing user authorization**. No production SQL,
evaluator behavior, root verifier, or reviewed T4 contract was modified.

Route: `T5.1-EVAL | Sol | max | attempt 1`.

The reviewed production interface is two bound table SQL macros:
`DOOM_R2_PORTAL_HITS(p_session VARCHAR2)` and
`DOOM_R2_SECTOR_INTERVALS(p_session VARCHAR2)`. The former retains every ordered
R1 analytic hit, including incompatible overlaps and rows after termination; the
latter exposes only the active contiguous sector intervals. The full fixed column
contract is recorded in `evaluator/t5.1/README.md`.

The independent analytic oracle establishes these semantics:

- exact stable order `(hit_t, linedef_id, seg_id, facing_side)`;
- a hit advances only when its facing sector is the current active sector;
- opening bottom is `max(floors)`, opening top is `min(ceilings)`, and a
  nonpositive opening is closed;
- lower and upper pieces are directed from facing sector toward the opposite
  sector, so stepping down does not invent a near-facing lower wall;
- an active open hit transitions to the opposite sector, a closed or one-sided
  hit terminates, and all later analytic rows remain retained but inactive;
- an unterminated path ends with one final interval at configured far distance.

Eight purpose-built mini-maps cover windows, chained steps, open and closed
doors, coincident overlaps, endpoint vertex ties, three nested portals, solid
termination, and an open far interval. The vertex fixture deliberately freezes a
zero-length sector interval between two compatible equal-depth transitions.
Translation is byte-identical; reflection preserves the semantic timeline while
flipping only the facing-side bit within the inherited numeric tolerances.
