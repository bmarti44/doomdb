Status: **PASS — production collision corrected, frozen evaluators unchanged**

## Defect and correction

E1M1 sector 81 is a sixteen-unit-deep door throat bounded by portals 483 at
`y=2080` and 442 at `y=2096`, with one-sided jambs 482/484 at `x=640/768`.
Its depth is smaller than the radius-16 player's diameter. When the door was
open, ordinary zero-radial endpoint contact at the wall continuation blocked a
single sweep that crossed both portals.

The endpoint-cap rule remains conservative for ordinary geometry: negative
radial derivative enters a cap, and zero derivative remains a blocking
tangency. `DOOM_THIN_PORTAL_GRAZE` supplies one topology-based exception only
when all of the following are true:

- the candidate is a one-sided jamb endpoint;
- that vertex is shared with a currently open, two-sided portal whose
  supporting line the center sweep crosses;
- a second, parallel, currently open portal is connected through either sector
  of the first portal; and
- that paired portal is less than one player diameter from the jamb vertex.

This recognizes the sector-81 paired throat without hard-coded line or sector
IDs. Ordinary endpoint tangency, inward jamb overlap, segment-body collision,
and dynamic opening/step tests remain blocking.

A later retained T8 replay exposed a second defect. At sequence 784 the center
moved from `(1086.6302410964334,2095.647841757096)` in BSP sector 19 by
`(-22.19263752515436,+4.414390068527088)` to sector 74 with zero contacts,
despite crossing no finite two-sided portal. `DOOM_PORTAL_TRANSITION_OK` now
requires a changed destination sector to be connected by one or two finite,
currently open portals actually crossed by the center sweep. Two hops preserve
sub-diameter throats. The exact sector-81 endpoint graze remains admitted only
when the trajectory is radius-tangent to the qualifying thin-portal endpoint.

## Public-prefix equivalence

After the independently owned tic-order correction, the production
`DOOM_TIC_TX.APPLY_BATCH` replay matches the reviewed opening prefix through
sequence 163, including positions, health, and kills. This collision work did
not alter the transaction or history packages.

The transition guard is bounded before dynamic topology evaluation: portal
candidates must involve the start/destination sectors and overlap the finite
movement bounding box; thin-tangent endpoints must overlap the radius-expanded
box, and the helper runs only after exact tangent math succeeds. The live
state-only benchmark completes 100 ordinary, 25 rejected phantom, and 25
thin-tangent moves in 1.97–2.11 seconds. The exact public sequence-163 prefix
completes in 31.36 seconds when run alone.
