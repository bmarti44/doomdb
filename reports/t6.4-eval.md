## Frozen contract

The internal boundary is `DOOM_HISTORY`, with unique non-overloaded procedures
`CAPTURE_TIC`, `SAVE_GAME`, `LOAD_GAME`, `REWIND_TO_TIC`, `START_REPLAY`, and
`STEP_REPLAY`. `DOOM_TIC_TX` delegates capture after authoritative tic effects
and before response construction. Transaction ownership remains outside the
history package.

Commands, gameplay events, and audio events are immutable lineage-qualified
hash chains. Save/load and rewind create deterministic new continuation lineages
while retaining the old future and monotonically advancing the global command
sequence. Logical state/frame hashes intentionally exclude persistence metadata,
so an identical continuation matches uninterrupted play. Snapshot envelopes bind
complete authoritative state, schema, lineage, sequence frontier, command/event
heads, state hash, and blob hash.

The reviewed visible snapshot interval is four logical tics, stored as relational
configuration `HISTORY_SNAPSHOT_INTERVAL=4`. Every save also creates or reuses an
exact-tic checkpoint. Reconstruction verifies the greatest trusted snapshot no
later than the target and the exact remaining ordered command/event range.
Replay state belongs to its cursor, advances exactly one tic per step, checks both
state and frame hashes, and is independent of mutable live state.

Coverage rejects corrupt snapshots, commands, events, state hashes, frame hashes,
omissions, reordered commands, duplicate event ordinals, incomplete ranges,
invalid slots/ranges, unknown replay ids, destructive rewind, lineage reuse,
wall-clock checkpoints, dynamic SQL, autonomous transactions, and evaluator
coupling. Save/load and arbitrary-tic rewind continuations are compared at every
tic with uninterrupted play; bounded and new-game replay final hashes are fixed.
