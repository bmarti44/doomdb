## Candidate contract

The internal boundary is the unique `DOOM_AUDIO.EMIT(session,tic)` procedure at
Appendix F step 11. It derives sound/music solely by joining authoritative
`GAME_EVENTS` to `DOOM_AUDIO_EVENT_DEF`, resolves seeded database assets, and
writes session/lineage-scoped tuples ordered by logical event keys with dense
per-tic ordinals. `AUDIO_EVENTS` must carry the relational
`(asset_kind,asset_name)` identity so both sound and music are provenance-bound.

The browser boundary is `createAudioPresenter({endpoint})`. It strictly validates
and advances the `(tic,ordinal)` cursor, rejects duplicate/reordered/malformed
input, shares one GET_ASSET/decode promise per asset, queues before consent, and
schedules once only after a user gesture resumes `AudioContext`. Gain and pan are
the only client conversions. Audio cannot inspect or alter gameplay inputs,
state, frames, hashes, transport results, or tic timing.
